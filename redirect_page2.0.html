Introduction to the Hindustaani Vocal Music and Basics

A traditional form of classical music originating from northern India. It is characterized by a system of melodic scales called ragas, rhythmic cycles known as talas, and improvisation within a structured framework. Performances typically include alaap (slow-tempo introduction), bandish (fixed composition), and taans (fast melodic patterns).

Alankaars and more

Musical ornamentation and embellishments applied to both melodic and rhythmic elements. It involves techniques such as slides, grace notes, and oscillations, enhancing the expressiveness of the music. In melodic contexts, alankaar is often used during the alaap section to decorate the basic notes of a raga.
